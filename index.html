Our Research

Research Gap Analysis

Despite global advancements in AI and geospatial technologies, there are key gaps in adapting these innovations to Sri Lanka’s unique hiking environments

1. Limited Local CNN Models for Sri Lankan Mountain Recognition

There is currently no CNN-based model trained on Sri Lankan mountain data, making global image recognition tools ineffective for local use. The lack of region-specific datasets and offline capability creates a major gap in real-time mountain identification tailored to Sri Lanka’s hiking environment.

01

Inadequate Trail Mapping for Local Hiking Conditions

Current hiking apps lack features like real-time updates, community collaboration, and offline access, making them unsuitable for Sri Lanka’s remote and low-connectivity trails. This highlights the need for a locally adapted, collaborative mapping system that can dynamically reflect changing trail conditions and function without constant internet access.

02

Lack of Personalized and Context-Aware Travel Time Estimation

Existing travel time models are generic and fail to account for Sri Lanka’s varied terrains, weather, and individual hiker differences. There are no AI-based or behavior-driven models locally that use real-time data to predict hiking time and distance, creating a major gap in providing accurate, personalized route planning and ensuring hiker safety.

03

Absence of Reliable Off-Grid Emergency Communication Systems

Current emergency systems rely on mobile networks, which are often unavailable in Sri Lanka’s remote hiking areas. Although low-cost solutions like Wi-Fi mesh networks exist, they haven’t been implemented or adapted for local hiking conditions. This creates a critical safety gap, leaving hikers without reliable communication during emergencies.

Research Focus

Research Problem & Solution

Understanding the Challenge

How can we accurately classify pests and diseases in coconut palms and provide real-time surveillance to stakeholders?

Addressing the critical challenges in coconut cultivation through advanced technological solutions.

Weligama Coconut Leaf Wilt Disease (WCLWD) and Coconut Caterpillar Infestation (CCI) are among the most severe threats to coconut cultivation in Sri Lanka. The impact is staggering, with approximately 300,000 palms already removed due to WCLWD and an additional 60,000 requiring identification for removal. The situation is further complicated by CCI, where defoliation exceeding 30% leads to significant economic losses. The key to effective management lies in early identification and establishing efficient communication channels between growers and agricultural professionals.

Our Innovative Solution

PeakFinder introduces a comprehensive mobile and web-based platform designed to combat WCLWD and CCI through cutting-edge technology. Our solution addresses the critical challenge of accurate disease identification by implementing advanced Deep Learning techniques, specifically Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN), to analyze leaf symptoms with unprecedented precision.

The platform serves a wide range of stakeholders, including coconut growers, estate managers, researchers at the Coconut Research Institute (CRISL), and Coconut Development Officers (CDOs). By simply capturing a photograph of affected leaves, users can instantly receive accurate diagnoses, while our system automatically extracts geolocation data to create a comprehensive disease tracking network.

For WCLWD, our system evaluates multiple symptoms to calculate disease severity, while for CCI, we employ sophisticated computer vision techniques including OpenCV and YOLO object detection to count caterpillars and assess infestation levels. The integration of MASK R-CNN enables precise calculation of infected areas, providing valuable data for progression analysis.

Our real-time notification system ensures immediate alerts for detected infections, enabling rapid response from agricultural authorities. The platform's mapping capabilities visualize infection patterns and predict potential risk zones, empowering proactive measures to protect unaffected areas. This comprehensive approach not only addresses current infections but also works to prevent future outbreaks through data-driven insights and early warning systems.

Our Goals

Research Objectives

Key objectives driving our research and development efforts

Development of a Real-Time Mountain Recognition System

The project aims to develop a real-time mountain recognition system for Sri Lanka using deep learning, specifically the MobileNetV2 model, optimized for mobile devices. Users can point their smartphones at a mountain to instantly receive its name, history, and other relevant details. This mobile application is designed to enhance the hiking experience for hikers, tourists, and students by providing interactive, hands-free educational information without relying on manual input or GPS.

01

Geolocation and Mapping System Development

The second objective is to develop an interactive mapping system tailored for hikers, featuring detailed trail maps, terrain types, POIs, and real-time updates. It will support route sharing and collaborative mapping, enabling users to contribute and update trail information. The system will also provide personalized hiking recommendations based on user preferences and environmental data. Real-time integration of weather, hazards, and trail conditions will ensure safer and more informed hiking experiences, especially in remote areas.

02

Geospatial Data Analysis for Distance and Travel Time Estimation

The third objective is to implement geospatial data analysis to accurately estimate hiking distance and travel time based on real-time environmental conditions, terrain, and user-specific factors like fitness, experience, and equipment load. Unlike traditional static models, this system will use predictive machine learning algorithms to deliver dynamic, personalized travel time estimates. It will continuously improve by learning from user feedback and new data, ensuring more precise predictions over time.

03

Wi-Fi Mesh Networks for Hiking Systems

The fourth objective is to develop a Wi-Fi mesh network protocol for hikers, enabling device-to-device communication in remote areas without internet or mobile networks. This ad-hoc network will support emergency alerts like SOS signals, prioritize low-latency and self-healing capabilities, and adapt to changing terrain. The system will be tested across various environments to ensure reliability, ultimately enhancing safety and communication during hiking expeditions.

Our Process

Methodology

A comprehensive, modular approach integrating deep learning for mountain recognition, interactive mapping, geospatial prediction models, and Wi-Fi mesh communication to deliver a real-time, intelligent hiking assistance system.

Figure 2. High Level Architecture of the system

This research adopted a mixed-methods approach combining experimental prototype development with both qualitative and quantitative evaluations. The system was modularized into four functional components to address the comprehensive requirements of the proposed Hiking Assistance System (HAS):

  • Real-Time Mountain Recognition
  • Geolocation and Mapping
  • Geospatial Data Analysis
  • Wireless Mesh Communication

A. Real-Time Mountain Recognition

A MobileNetV2 model was trained on curated and augmented Sri Lankan mountain images. The model was optimized using the Adam optimizer and deployed via Flask API, integrated with a React Native frontend. Performance was validated using accuracy, precision, recall, and confusion matrix.

B. Geolocation and Mapping

An interactive map was built using React Native, Firebase, and Google Maps API. It features route planning, real-time trail updates, and collaborative mapping. A modified Dijkstra’s algorithm determines optimal paths based on trail data and user inputs.

C. Geospatial Data Analysis

Environmental and user-specific data were collected and processed. Machine learning models were evaluated, with the Random Forest Regressor selected for its accuracy. Integrated with OpenWeather API, the model provides dynamic predictions of hiking distance and time.

D. Wireless Mesh Communication Network

A Wi-Fi mesh network was built using ESP8266/ESP32 NodeMCU devices for peer-to-peer communication in remote areas. The network enables low-latency emergency alerts and is self-healing to maintain functionality in rugged environments. Firebase was used for data syncing.
